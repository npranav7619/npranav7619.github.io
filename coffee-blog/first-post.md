**Coffee Bean Choices**


Coffee enthusiasts often find themselves captivated by the rich
world of coffee beans, each type offering a unique flavor
and characteristics. In this article, we will delve into the distinct features of
four different coffee beans: 

Arabica, Robusta, Excelsa and Liberica.

**Arabica** stands as the most widely used and cultivated coffee bean
globally, contributing to almost 60-70% of coffee production.
Known for its mild flavor and aromatic qualities,
Arabica beans are often favored for their complex taste

**Robusta** beans are recognized for their higher caffeine content
and a hint of chocolate flavor. However, some describe the
taste as somewhat flat or possessing an almost burnt quality.

**Excelsa coffee**, previously categorized as a distinct variant,
has recently been reclassified as a Liberica variant by scientists.
These beans offer a lighter aroma and lower caffeine
content compared to Arabica and Robusta.

**Liberica coffee** has earned a reputation for its controversial and
polarizing nature, primarily due to its wild inconsistency

**Coffee Choices**

*black* - the simplest of all...fresh coffee beans brewed with hot steaming water

*espresso* - is crafted using finely ground beans and a higher grounds-to-water ratio compared to regular coffee. This process yields a denser, more concentrated liquid with a more robust flavor.

*latte* - this traditional beverage usually consists of one-third espresso and two-thirds steamed milk, crowned with a delicate layer of foam.

*cappuccino* - this coffee creation, rooted in espresso, bears resemblance to a latte, yet distinguishes itself with a denser frothy top layer. The customary proportion comprises equal parts espresso, steamed milk, and foam.

*americano* - this is espresso but diluted.

*mocha* - are espresso-based drinks, featuring chocolate and whipped cream instead of foam, provide a delightful blend, offering a rich and satisfying experience with every sip.
